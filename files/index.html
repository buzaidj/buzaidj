Mobile ordering

I designed, implemented, and improved upon a mobile ordering platform used for an entire year by my social living group due to in-person ordering being unsafe. I talked with our chef and used some of my restaurant experience to come up with a good solution. A member fills out a Google form, that is read by a Raspberry Pi and formatted and printed by a receipt printer.

My social living house has a meal plan that serves 90 members lunch and dinner five days a week. Prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, lunch orders were done in short order fashion. Each meal plan member would tell our chef what they wanted for lunch. Dinner was self-serve. In the summer before the fall 2020 semester, I realized that this ordering method would not work. Influenced by my experience working as a cook in restaurants, I decdied an ordering form system similar to DoorDash (for pickup only and without a payment system). After talking with our chef, I decided using a printer printing tickets for orders would make a good solution.

On the user side I used a Google Forms form. It is easy for our chef to edit on his tablet to modify. Also, it is easy to read entries from a Raspberry Pi in the kitchen, with calls the Google Sheets API to read a submitted form submitted in a sheet that logs responses. Each of these form entries is validated by checking to make sure the same order wasn't already submitted (a common Google Forms bug). Then they are formatted and printed on a Epson thermal receipt printer, like in a restaurant setting.

Node.Spotify

I created a JavaScript app that lets a Spotify user generate a directed graph of their top artists to visualize their listening. These nodes of this graph are the users 50 top artists of the past six months and there is an edge from artist a1 to artist a2 if a2 is in the related artists of a1. I learned how authorization flows work, what HTML requests are and how to use AJAX to make them, and how to use jQuery to listen for page activity. In the process I learned how to use JavaScript.
